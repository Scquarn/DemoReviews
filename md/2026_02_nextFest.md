### 1

Starting off with [Romestead](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4407550/Romestead_Demo/). A game about, you guessed it champ, rebuilding Rome. The game starts off with a well animated intro about how Rome fell and now it's your duty to rebuild it.  
As many of these crafting games this one also starts with character creation. There are some basic choices like gender, hair and colors but that's about it. After that you create a word, it generates the god seed for a moment and then we can start the game.  
First thing we meet is a ghost which is acting as the guide of this game. It gives you a small quest to craft the basic tools.  
The game could best be described as a top down Terraria. Crafting the tools and some houses I'm starting a small village. You can also build defenses and traps to defend the town against raids.  
At night zombies start attacking. Like in Terraria, in this one you also start off really weak and slow but there are quite a lot of inventory slots for equipment, so I think you can get a lot more mobile and stronger later in the game. At the start however it feels quite bad you can't even run, only dash a little bit on a few seconds cooldown but that's it.  
The game seems well made and I can see it being fun, but since the tutorial only explains the most basic tool crafting, I'm a bit lost in what to do next. Could be a game for you if you want to do some crafty crafty. Also, Rome is cool I suppose.

### 2

Jumping in next is [The Eternal Life of Goldman](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4315490/The_Eternal_Life_of_Goldman_Demo/). A classic jump and run but hold it, this art style is absolutely stunning. Insane pixel art, detailed, colorful, an absolute joy to look at.  
The game itself is told as a story, think princess bride. A woman is telling a kid the story about Goldman, an elderly man with his trusty cane.  
Did I mention that the art is absolutely amazing?  
The mechanics are fun, too. Instead of upgrades you can change the cane Goldman is using to jump. Adjust the top to cling to objects or change out the base to get a higher jump. Spikes on the ceiling and a high jump would damage you? Better adapt that cane!  
And it's not easy either. The first level you breeze through no problem but then it quickly gets tougher. There are also secret chests you can find everywhere which urge you to explore and to try the harder paths.  
I really like this one. Only downside are the NPCs you encounter as they are a bit too talkative for my liking. Pages upon pages of dialogue when I just want to keep going instead of getting the Hiroshima of lore drops. Definitely check this one out if you like jump and runs. If not, take a look for the art alone.

### 3

On our search for the vampire we find
[Vampire Crawlers: The Turbo Wildcard from Vampire Survivors](Vampire_Crawlers_The_Turbo_Wildcard_from_Vampire_Survivors) in the next coffin. Another game from the makers of the indie game plague that is Vampire Survivors.  
First off: Is this another game that will have a million clones like Vampire Survivors? No.  
Is this game still good? Maybe.  
In Vampire Crawlers you create a deck in classic roguelike fashion like in Slay the Spire. However the mechanics are quite simple.
Play attack cards to deal damage. Play armor cards to gain armor against enemy attacks. The trick in game are the combos and the persistent buff system.  
Each cards costs between zero and three mana. If you play them in order the next card gets buffed. So if you start of with a zero card then a one card and so on, each one of them will be quite a lot stronger.  
Buffs you gain, like for example one that gives you multiple projectiles persist between the turns but not between battles. So every time you start a battle you start weak but ramp up.  
The battles themselves happen on a very basic map. There you can find enemies, consumables like chests, secrets, or the boss of the stage. Defeating the boss allows you to advance to the next floor.  
Move on the map, find enemies, combo them and then finally challenge the boss. Game is simple but you can definitely do some cool stuff, especially as each of your cards can be upgrades with a gem. These add stuff like extra armor, double damage or extra mana once you use them.  
Game is neat but it doesn't blow me away. Not sure if I would play this for a longer than a few rounds. Could be a good pickup however if it’s as cheap as Vampire Survivors.

### 4

Sliding in next is [Super Meat Boy 3D](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3288210/Super_Meat_Boy_3D/). The classic jump and run made by the Edmund McMillen.  
This time however, we are jumping in 3D. That's it, that's the gimmick. Even the levels reminded me of the original game, at least at the start. Later on they swap it up, but maybe this is only for the demo to show off some new content. It would be cool to see all of the original levels reimagined in 3D.  
The game looks neat but the controls feel much harder than in the original. In fact after some digging, I found out that the root cause for this is that it has a 45° grid snapping by default for some strange reason. Turning that off in the settings changes everything. Suddenly turning and jumping feels as smooth as you would expect it from a Meat Boy game.  
The game is fun but in my personal opinion they should let meat boy rest. He has suffered enough already. Oh and go play the original if you haven't. It's one of the best jump and runs ever made. Absolute banger game.

### 5 Tombwater Demo

It's high noon somewhere in the world. It certainly is in [Tombwater](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3547600/Tombwater_Demo/). A 2D pixel art game with a Western style that has clearly been inspired by dark souls.  
Unfortunately they did not copy the master Miyazaki but Tanimura instead. Game has controls that feel choppy and make it hard to control the character. You can't move for a while after you attack. Where is the fluidity? Did the devs ever try this out themselves?  
There is also the scholar of the first sin enemy spam that makes the game even worse to play on top of that. In addition to all that every enemy is also way too tanky. Kiting with these controls for minutes isn’t fun.  
Combine all of these points and you get a game that is not fun to play. The idea is cool, Western and Eldritch Horror blend together well, but you need more than a cool theme to impress.

### 6

Did you ever wish that your backpack was a spaceship? Well, now you can live out your dreams in [Down with the Ship](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3668060/Down_with_the_Ship_Demo/).  
This is a backpack battles clone, but instead of expanding and filling up your backpack you are building a spaceship.  
Buy new ship parts, cannons, energy generators, crew mates and the like and puzzle them together to make a ship that fights against ships made by other players.  
There are a lot of mechanics like, weapon types, fuel, energy levels, crew mates, mines, defenses, hull strength. It's certainly not easy to create a good ship but it's fun. Not a bad one!

### 7

Flying in next is [Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3470630/Monster_Hunter_Stories_3_Twisted_Reflection_Trial_Version/).  
The Monster Hunter Stories games are like Pokemon but with the big lizards from the Monster Hunter series instead. Find and tame some Monsties and use them in battle against others.  
Like Pokemon this has turn based combat but it's much much more complex. There are normal attacks, skills and monster moves. Each one of those has three types. Strength, tactical and speed. While strength beats tactical, it counters speed which counters strength to come full circle. Picking the right type against the right enemy is key to win the fight.  
Besides that there are also specials moves once you topple an enemy or riding moves once you fill up a special meter in the fight. It's a bit complex but fun.  
From what I heard this game also has a large set of available monsters, so it's tight on that aspect too. Graphics are in a more animated style and as usual from other Monster Hunter games there is a lot of customization for your character, including layered armor.  
Some might say that it only being singleplayer is a downside but not everything has to be multiplayer. The demo is quite extensive and has enough content for at least ten hours.
Game is great but for me personally not good enough for the seventy euros opening price. Will probably pick it up on a sale however to finally try one of these out in depth.

### 9

Psst. Let me tell you about [EverSiege: Untold Ages](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4210690/EverSiege_Untold_Ages_Demo/). A game that really surprised me with it's core gameplay concept.
It's a mix of base building, a Moba and Vampire Survivors. You control a hero unit and have to defend your base against an enemy champion.  
Each night the enemy spawns waves of minions that travel along a path towards your base. You can construct towers along that path to fight them together with your hero.  
While not fighting the enemy your can clear neutral enemies to level and get stronger. There are also certain points of interest that upgrade your own units that spawn in waves along the path to combat the enemy champion. It's a delicate act between defending, leveling and upgrading the base.  
Between games there are also quite a lot of customization for your hero, like the spells you use and it seems like there will be a few different heroes in the full game to pick from.  
Unfortunately while the idea is great, there are some glaring issues. Controlling the hero unit feels just down right awful. Despite the game offering different movement methods like click to move or WASD, none work properly. If you walk into a wall the character simply gets stuck for a while before you can move again.  
Neutral enemies use a lot of circle based attacks but dodging them is simply impossible because you are either to slow or the character doesn't move where you want them to.  
In addition to that it never feels like you can get ahead of the curve. At every point during a game you feel weak and underpowered. Could be I'm just bad at it, but maybe the power curve is a bit off here.  
Overall this game reminds me of a custom Warcraft 3 map like back in the days. It does have potential but is held back by too many issues to be recommended at the moment. Keep at it devs, you can make it good, I believe.

### 10

Do you know the arcade game where a thing goes back and forth to push down coins? You throw in coins at the back and hope it pushes the stack of coins at the front down.  
This is that.  
Add a raccoon theme on top of that, unique coins, active items and artifacts like in Balatro or other roguelike deck builders and you get this game.  
It is well made. The vibe is there. But I ask: How fun is throwing coins in and seeing the machine push them and other coins down the drain? Let me answer it for you. Not at all. In fact it's quite boring. Waiting for coins to be pushed can only ever be so satisfying no mater how crazy the items get.  
Really well made, but boring by design. Balatro fans will probably gobble this up however. Vibe gaming.

### 12

Ambatu? No it's [Armatus](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3660710/Armatus/). A roguelike arena shooter. Think doom but without the cool movement.

### 13

Hitting [Rock Bottom](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4297840/Rock_Bottom/). I thought this was another Terraria like but to my surprise it's an incremental game.  
Play as a little goblin and head down the elevator to mine metals. The first level has gold, the second cobalt, the third something else I didn't bother to check.  
You have an Stardew Valley like energy system and every block you mine uses a bit of that stamina. Here is where the incremental part comes in. With the metals and rocks you mine you can upgrade your character. More stamina, faster mining, special lightning effects on mining a block, you know it.  
Numbers must go up.  
There are no enemies or anything. It's only a chill goblin mining ore. Slow and boring.

### 14

Really? Another? I'm incrementing the demos with [IncreKnight](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4305480/IncreKnight/).

Buy upgrades to slay enemies faster, advance further, delay the dungeon collapse, the usual incremental gameplay.  
To my surprise this one has boss fights. After clearing the first few rooms of the demo the first one shows up and it was quite the surprise and change in gameplay as I actually had to doge some attacks and 'play' the game.  
A unique twist on the whole incremental formula other than that it's your usual run of the mill numbers go up game. Nothing new under the sun. Oh yeah, the art is alright in this one.

### 17

As the name suggest everything in this game is made with red pixel art. The resolution is quite low so it's hard to tell what you are looking at but after a while it's not that bad.
In this game the character shoots bullets you modify with upgrades from their gun to combat the waves of enemies. Each bullet upgrade has two paths that can be taken with three steps making it six total upgrades per ability. The longer the game goes on the more guns get added that shoot the modified bullets.  
Unique is also that every boss enemy in the later part of a round spawns a magnet that collects all XP so optimal movement to collect the drops isn't as important as in other Vampire Survivor likes and dodging can be the main focus.  
The boss fights were okay, but nothing new. Pretty much copies from the first map of Vampire Survivors.  
Game is a decent adaption of the formula but not enough to be worth playing.
