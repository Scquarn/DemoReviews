Stop. Take a breath. Is that air? Are you sitting in a chair? Nobody reads this to be fair. But just in case you do, here is a thought: Do you still know what is real?
That last trip to the grocery store. The talk with the cashier. Products you brought home. Money you spent. Food you made and ate. Is that real?
That last game you played. The last run you lost. The last match you won. The enemies you faced. The people you played with. The fun you had. Is that real?
That last thing you read. The last book you held. The last character you liked. The last story you remember. The many words that passed by. Is that real?
That last message you wrote. The last interaction you had. The last exchange with another stranger on the internet. The fleeting way you passed into their life for a second. Is that real?
Today all the things we experience day by day become less and less physical in nature but are they any less real? Are these words you read here less real than that shower you took? Is there a difference? Does there have to be?
Look at the news. Thousand channels and two thousand different versions. All of them about the same thing but all of them saying something different and at the same time they are all identical. Lies or are all of them real?
Isn't it curious how nobody experiences an event in the same way. Take this text here, despite being brought into reality by me pushing my fingers on the different letters on my keyboard and having an indisputable physical form right in front of your eyes, no two persons will understand it in the same way. A different meaning for everyone. A different piece in their reality.
And with a million channels, digital most of them, physical others, we take in an uncountable amount of new pieces and add them to our realities. What is the truth any more and does it even matter?
Let's say you see a video about a topic that may even interest or much more likely you don't care about by a stranger. Are they an expert? Who knows. Is their input valuable? Is it even correct what they say? It is impossible to tell. Still, even if they lie, if their words and thoughts only grace your reality for a split moment while scrolling past it, they will taint your world. Add on to your reality. Constructing their own room and taking a part away from you. Changing the way you perceive what is real. What matters. They reshape your reality with out you even knowing it.
So, what's the point of all these words? Adding onto your own isn't bad. In fact, I think it's the only way to not rot away. To take in new pieces and to add on to your own. To built and built until your reality becomes beautiful.
Like strokes on a canvas. At first it's blank but with each one a painting is being made. A reality isn't as simple as that. It goes beyond the Edges of the Canvas.
A craft made by uncountable things. All of them coming together to form something special.
You.
So what is the point of all these words? To make you conscious? To make you see? No. I'm not so presumptuous to think I could change you with a few words. To tell you the truth, there is no deeper reason. None besides me offering a few reviews of great demos that you can add to your reality.

Demos

Next Fest was in February and once more you are standing there. Crying like a kid waiting for it's parents. Did he forget to do it this time? Did he abandon this project? These thoughts and more like them take over your brain. And it's true. A day may come when I will stop doing demo reviews and stop writing these things, but it is not this day.
Same new format, new demos, new layout. The new nuggets of human creativity condensed into a single, easy to digest format.
This time there were some amazing games. So many times I kept playing because the games were simply fun. I hope you will check out some of these games from the best Next Fest yet.

1

Starting off with Romestead. A game about, you guessed it champ, rebuilding Rome. The game start off with a nice intro about how Rome fell and now it's your duty to rebuild it.
As many of these crafting games this one also starts with you first making your character. There are some basic choices like gender, hair, colors but that's about it. Next you create a word, it generates the god seed for a moment and here we go loading into the game.
We meet a ghost who is acting as the guide of this game. It gives you a small quest to craft the basic tools.
The game could best be described as a top down Terraria. Crafting the tools and some houses I'm starting a small village. You can also build defenses and traps to defend the town against raids.
At night zombies start attacking. Yeah, thinking about it further, it really is top down Terraria and like in that game you start off really weak and slow. There are a lot of inventory slots so I think you can get a lot more mobile and stronger later in the game. However the start feels quite bad as you can't even run, only dash a little bit and that's it.
The game doesn't seem bad, but since the tutorial only explains the most basic tool crafting, I'm a bit lost in what to do next. Could be fun if you want to do some crafty crafty. Also, Rome is cool I suppose.

2

Jumping in next is The Eternal Life of Goldman. A classic jump and run but hold it, this art style is absolutely stunning.
The game is told as a story, think princess bride. A woman is telling a kid the story about Goldman, an elderly man with his trusty cane.
Did I mention that the art is absolutely amazing?
The mechanics are fun, too. Instead of upgrades you can change the cane Goldman is using for jumping. Adjust the top to cling to objects or change out the base to get a higher jump? Spikes on the ceiling and a high jump would damage you? Better adapt that cane!
And it's not easy either. The first level you breeze through no problem but then it quickly gets tougher. There are also secret chests you can find everywhere.
I really like it. Only downside are the NPCs you encounter as they are a bit too talkative for my liking. Pages upon pages of dialogue when I just want to keep jumping. Definitely check this one out if you like these games.

3

On our search for the vampire we find, Vampire Crawlers: The Turbo Wildcard from Vampire Survivors in the next coffin. Another game from the makers of the plague that is Vampire Survivors.
First off, is this another game that will have a million clones like Vampire Survivors? No.
Is this game still good? Maybe.
In this you create a deck in classic roguelike style like Slay the Spire. However the mechanics are quite simple. Play attack cards to deal damage. Play armor cards to gain armor against enemy attacks. The trick in this is the combo and persistent buff system.
Each cards costs between zero and three mana. If you play them in order the next card gets buffed. So start of with a zero card, than a one card and so on.
Buffs you gain, like for example multiple projectiles persist between the turns but not between battles. So every time you start a battle you start weak and ramp up.
The battles themselves happen on a very basic map. There you can find enemies, consumables like chests, secrets, or the boss of the stage. Defeating the boss allows you to advance to the next.
Move on the map, find enemies, combo them. Game is simple. You can definitely do some cool stuff, especially as each of your cards can be upgrades with a gem. They add stuff like extra armor, double damage or extra mana once you use them.
The game is neat but it doesn't blow me away. Not sure if I would play this for a longer than a few rounds.

4

Sliding in next is Super Meat Boy 3D. The classic jump and run made by the Edmund McMillen, that little fucker.
This time we are jumping in 3D. That's it, that's the gimmick. Even the level remind me of the OG, at least at the start. Later on they swap it up, but maybe this is only for the demo. It would be cool to see all of the OG level reimagined in 3D.
The game looks neat but the controls feel much harder than in the original. In fact the root cause of this is that it has a 45° grid snapping for some reason. Turning that off in the settings changes everything. Suddenly turning and jumping feels as smooth as you would expect it from a Meat Boy game.
The game is fun but let meat boy rest. He has suffered enough already. Oh and go play the original if you haven't. It's one of the best jump and runs ever made.

5

It's high noon somewhere in the world. It certainly is in Tombwater. A 2D pixel art game with a Western style that has clearly been inspired by dark souls.
Unfortunately they did not copy the master Miyazaki but Tanimura instead. Game has controls that feel choppy and honestly not even fun to control. You can't for a while when you attack. Where is the fluidity?
There is also the scholar of the first sin enemy spam that makes the game even worse to play. On top of that every enemy is really tanky.
Combine all of that and you get a game that is just not fun to play. The idea is cool, Western and Eldritch Horror seem to blend together well, but you need more than a cool theme to impress.

6

Did you ever wish that your backpack was a spaceship? Well you can now live out your dreams with Down with the Ship.
This is a backpack battles clone but instead of filling up your backpack you are building a spaceship. Buy new ship parts, cannons, energy generators, crew mates and the like and puzzle them together to make a spaceship to fight against the ships other players have created.
There are a lot of mechanics, weapon types, fuel, energy levels, crew mates, mines, defenses, hull strength. It's certainly not easy to create a good ship but it's fun. Not a bad one!

7

Flying in next is Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection.
The Monster Hunter Stories games are like Pokemon but with the big lizards from the Monster Hunter series instead. Find and tame some Monsties and use them in battle against others.
Like Pokemon this has turn based combat but it's much much more complex. There are normal attacks, skills and monster moves. Each one of those has three types. Strength, tactical and speed. While strength beats tactical, it counters speed which counters strength to come full circle. Picking the right type against the enemy is key to win the fight.
Besides that there are also specials moves once you topple an enemy or riding moves once you fill up a special meter in the fight. It's complex but fun.
From what I heard this game also has a big set of available monsters, so it's tight. Graphics are nice too and as usual from other Monster Hunter games there is a lot of customization for your character, including layered armor.
Some might say that it only being singleplayer is a downside but not everything has to be multiplayer. The demo is quite extensive and has enough content for at least ten hours. Game is great but not good enough for the seventy euros opening price. Will probably pick it up on a sale however to try finally try one of these out in depth.

8

Tap Tap Loot is spying on you. It's one of those games that sit at the bottom of the screen so you can 'play' it while doing other things.
First off: Why.
Secondly, the game sucks. For each input the game advances your little adventurer along a path of enemies and loot. Doing enough inputs advances you along the way. The loot is random and you get stronger the longer you play.
Now the kicker: What input does the game read? Literally every mouse click or keystroke you do even in other applications. So, this is just straight up a key logger, isn't it?

9

Psst. Let me tell you about EverSiege: Untold Ages. A game that really surprised me with it's core gameplay concept. It's a mix of base building, a Moba and Vampire Survivors. You control a hero unit and have to defend your base against an enemy champion.
Each night the enemy spawns waves of enemies that travel along a path towards your base. You can construct towers along the path to fight them together with your hero.
Your hero can clear neutral enemies to level and get stronger. There are also certain points of interest that upgrade your own units that spawn in waves to combat the enemy champion. It's a delicate act between defending, leveling and upgrading the base.
Between rounds there are also quite a lot of customization for your hero spells and it seems like there will be a few different heroes in the full game to pick from.
Unfortunately while the idea is great, there are some glaring issues. Controlling your hero feels just down right awful. Despite the game offering different movement methods like click to move or WASD none work properly. If you walk into a wall the character simply gets stuck for a while before you can move again.
Neutral enemies use a lot of circle based attacks but dodging them is simply impossible because you are either to slow or the character doesn't move where you want him to.
In addition to that it never feels like you can get ahead of the curve. At every point during a game you feel weak and underpowered. Could be I'm just bad at it, but maybe the power curve is a bit off here.
Overall this game reminds me of a custom Warcraft 3 map like back in the days. It has potential but is held back by too many issues to be recommended at the moment. Keep at it devs, you can do it, I believe.

10

Pushing in next is RACCOIN: Coin Pusher Roguelike. This game definitly copied the Balatro vibe and they did it well.
Do you know the arcade game where a thing goes back and forth to push down coins? You throw in coins at the back and hope it pushes the stack of coins at the front down.
This is that.
Add a raccon theme on top of that, unique coins, active items and artifacts like in Balatro or other rougelike deck builders and you get this.
It is well made. The vibe is there. But I ask: How fun is throwing in coins and seeing it push down other coins? Let me answer it for you. Not at all. In fact it's quite boring even. Waiting for coins to be pushed can only ever be so satisfying no mater how crazy the items get.
Really well made, but boring by design. Balatro stans will probably gobble this up tho. Vibe gaming.

Ambatu? No it's Armatus. A roguelike arena shooter. Think doom, without the cool movement.
You fight through different demon arenas and upgrade either your armor or your gun. You also get money to buy upgrades in shops you find along the way. After every arena you choose which path to take, but there is no map to plan ahead. The theme is post apocalyptic mixed with christian faith. I really like this as it reminds me a bit of trench crusade (Yeah i know it's a big shit storm and a scam, but check out the lore it's absolute peak).
Gunplay is alright, it checks all the boxes to not be called horrible but it's nothing that excites me either. The movement consists only out of running and a tiny little jump. Where is the craziness?
Not a game I would ever play but it's not trash either. However, looking back at this after one of the later demos, this feels really weak.

13

Hitting Rock Bottom. I thought this was a Terraria like but to my surprise it's an incremental game.
Play as a little goblin and head down the elevator to mine metals. The first level has gold, the second cobalt, the third something else I didn't bother to check.
You have an Stardew Valley like energy system and every block you mine uses a bit of that stamina. Here is where the incremental part comes in. With the metals and rocks you mine you can upgrade your character. More stamina, faster mining, special lightning effects on mining a block, you know it.
Numbers must go up.
There are no enemies or anything. It's only a chill goblin mining ore. Slow and boring.

14

Really? Another? I'm incrementing the demos with IncreKnight.
As the title suggest, it's another incremental game. Play as a knight, fight rooms of enemies by attacking in a circle around the character. Once the timer runs out the dungeon collapses and you have to start over.
Buy upgrades to slay enemies faster, advance further, delay the dungeon collapse, the usual incremental gameplay.
To my surprise this one has boss fights. After clearing the first few rooms of the demo the first one shows up and it was quite the surprise and change in gameplay as I actually had to doge some attacks and 'play' the game.
A unique twist on the whole thing but other than that it's your usual run of the mill incremental game. Nothing new under the sun. Oh yeah, the art is alright in this one.

Kloa - Child of the Forest is next.
A damn good looking 2D soulslike with nice pixel art graphics. However the start of the game is a bit slow. You are playing as Kloa, the child of the forest as the title suggest and you are on your way to avenge the heart of the forest or something like that.
Movement is meh, is it direction locked? It feels slow and unresponsive at times. It does look nice but it doesn't catch my attention at all.
I stopped playing before getting anywhere, because nothing was happening. Only the same enemies and pointless screens of forest to walk through. Maybe it picks up later but this wasn't for me despite clearly being made with a lot of care and detail.
Sorry devs.

Why is Ash Warden also an incremental game? I was looking for a relaxing tower defense and got this.
An incremental tower defense. There are three towers and only a single hero unit. Click the towers to transfer the hero to the tower to protect it. If one tower dies the game is over.
Of course the game is impossible to win on first try as this is where the incremental part comes in to play. Upgrade the tower resistances, hero damage, teleport speed, etc. The usual incremental game upgrade tree.
It's quite boring as each round you will without a doubt after a few minutes and every time it's the same enemies spawning in the same order. With the other incremental games before that was at least random every round.
This isn't it. Let the towers fall, I'm done.

18

Seeing red in Echoes of Red. The first true Vampire Survivors like in this Next Fest.
As the name suggest everything in this game is made with red pixel art. The resolution is quite low so it's hard to tell what you are even looking at but after a while it's not that bad. The character shoots bullets from a gun in this game to combat the waves of enemies. Each bullet upgrade has two paths that can be taken with three steps making it six total upgrades per ability. The longer the game goes on the more main guns get added that shoot the modified bullets.
Unique is also that every boss enemy in the later part of a round spawns a magnet that collects all XP so optimal movement to collect the drops isn't as important as in other Vampire Survivor likes and dodging can be made the main focus.
The boss fights were okay, but nothing new. Pretty much copies from the first map of Vampire Survivors.
Game is a decent adaption of the formula but not enough to be worth playing.

The next demo is Voidling Bound. A game made in the unreal engine and the two minutes of shader compile on start up better be worth it. Game starts with an insane long lore drop intro, like five minutes of pure lore being beamed into my brain. The gist of it, humans found bad stuff in the galaxy that corrupts everything and only voidlings can resist it. However voidlings are weak and as a wrangler we tame and bind with them to fight the corruption.
It's a roguelike creature collector. Get voidlings and upgrade their stats, abilities and mutate them into new forms to get the specific type of creature you want.
With the little voidling you then go on missions to fight the corruption. Plays kind of like a hero shooter, but the different missions are a bit too similar to each other.
The movement, shooting and bosses are fun though. The game isn't bad but is missing that little special something. There are multiple difficulties, maybe it gets more fun once you crank those up.

Did you know that crabs have evolved back into existence multiple times already? Everything is Crab: The Animal Evolution Roguelite is a tribute to crabness.
A mixture of Vampire Survivors and spore and honestly it's quite an amazing idea. Start of as a simple blue blob and gather upgrades like new limbs, tails, teeth, horns and the like to create the ultimate lifeforms. You don't only get XP from enemies but can also focus on eating fruits or corpses like a scavenger.
It a "one more run" type of game. One more try and you can evolve into the perfect creature and once you look at the clock you notice that it's already been an hour.
Love the synergies with the skills and stats and how the game looks. Cute pixel arts and the different creatures you make remind me of Isaac with his many unique forms you get by picking up the various items.
The start of a run can be a bit slow but it picks up the further you get. Like it.

Your move Gambonanza. Let's try this again, the mandatory chess game for this Next Fest.
This time it's chess crossed with the Balatro vibes. A chess Balatro. To be honest, this fucking slaps.
Place chess pieces each round to fight the enemy. Your pieces that get captured are lost forever. At any time you can only have three pieces on the board but if you lose one you can place another one down. Between the rounds there is a shop where you can not only buy more chess pieces but also gambits that are the run altering artifacts in this game.
For example I found one that lets me take another turn after a pawn captures an enemy piece.
But those are not all mechanics. There are of course also special chess pieces. One that can only be captured once it's the last on the board, one that polymorphs into a queen and many more.
Game is crazy and crazy good. Chess rules. Wishlist.

Hop in the Croak Demo.
A platformer where you play as a frog. Use the tongue to either drag towards a wall or repell and fly far if you are holding into the opposite direction of the wall.
Tongue wall jumps? Check.
Game is fun, graphics are smooth and movement feels greats. Celeste but with a frog.
Levels also get tricky quite fast. Another good game.

28

Going in circles in The Loopler. Another roguelike, but this time a bit more like Balatro.
You upgrade a race track with gates and items so your little race car can drive more loops with the different upgrades which increase either fuel capacity, fuel refills, the score needed to pass the level or boost to go faster.
Each level has a certain score that needs to be reached to continue to the next level. Think Balatro and the Ante that gets raised each time.
At the start the game is very basic but you quickly unlock more mechanics and things to buy in the shop. For example after the first won run I get gates that make the next one score double and one that spawns a ghost car for a few seconds which also scores like the real car.
The game is neat but once you find a combination that works the run is instantly won. Getting a few boost gates and fuel gates with a golden gate that gives you one coin once the car passes is enough to completely break it. Each item in the shop costs between five and twenty coins but when you get a few thousands after the first few rounds and can just always buy everything the shop kind of loses it's purpose.
There is also some meta progression that reminds a bit of an incremental game, so I'm sure the later runs will go absolute crazy. With a bit of balance fixing the game could be quite fun to mess around with.

The next one piqued my interest. The Piper of Dawn is unique.
Starting off, it's one of those that start you in Chinese but with some menu kong-fu it can be switched to English.
Gameplay is like a farming game. You are the new alchemist in town and have to plant, water, refine, enrich different crops into products and potions to fulfill the quests of the towns people.
Besides these mechanics there is an alchemy systems, upgrades, random buffs each day, your own shop, automation and more. The interesting part besides all of that is that this plays almost like a visual novel with the insane amount of dialogue that the different characters have. There's tavern girls, a wolf guy, death, an eldritch abomination, a skeleton in your attic and more.
Not only are the characters unique, they are quite fun to interact with. The story seems engaging enough that you want to know what happens next or who these people are and what the hell is even going on in this town.
Graphics are in anime style and quite well done.
Makes me want to keep playing just one more day to see what happens next. It's a wishlist for me.

Taking off with Steel Artery: Train City Builder.
A management game where you build a city on a train. Manage the different needs of the residents like food, waste, hygiene, labor, etc. You know what a city management game is.
In this however you build the city as a train. Add new wagons and build rooms and facilities on top. Once everything is together the city can take off and travel through the land.
Visit different places to get new residents, gather local resources, or solve quests.
Game is well made and the whole train thing is a cool twist on the formula. Recommended.

This one sucks.

38

From roguelike to roguelike. Archon Soul is very similar to Slay the Spire but this time with dice.
Choose a few spells as your attacks and roll three dice each round. Use the rolled dice to activate the spells. Some need even numbers others need odds. Some need a total number to be activated, others take any dice but can only activated a few times.
The dice themselves can be modified in two ways. First their faces, so you could create one that only rolls odd numbers. Then you can add special runes to a face to add special effects like adding armor, removing enemies armor, doubling effects, lifesteal and many more. Lastly you can also modify the materials of the dice. A hot dice can heal you after a battle while a metal one deals extra damage.
The deck building and other mechanics are crazy. Dice can also be used on enemies to activate a special mechanic. There are many different enemies with different effects so you have to be careful like in STS or you will end up with a super buffed enemy because he ate all your high rolls.
The bosses are also fun and quite challenging. My build was to summon a sandstorm that damages the enemy each turn and I would stack enough armor to survive any attacks. Unfortunately I encountered an enemy after the third boss that had an instant kill after a timer ran out and my sandstorm wasn't fast enough to kill it.
Very good game, it was a lot of fun and really reminded me of that Slay the Spire esque greatness. Check it out.

Too chaotic, too unpolished.

What if I told you that The Magus Circle was the last Vampire Survivors like of this Next fest?
This time with another unique twist. To attack you have to draw the correct symbols of you spells on the screen while moving around and dodging the waves of enemies.
A little spark for the lightning spell, an hourglass without a top for magic missiles. The stronger the spell the more complex the symbol.
The concept is cool but it gets annoying fast as you have to keep reapplying the different symbols for the spells to be active. Each one has a little timer and then it stops working until you trigger it again.

46

Luna Abyss coming in next and this is how you do it.
None of that boring and slow gameplay like in Armatus.
Singleplayer shooter, with controls that feel snappy as hell. Weapons are energy based with infinite ammo but due to them heating up it enforces weapon swapping like in Doom.
The different enemies release slow moving projectiles that you have to dodge.
Artstyle is a unique mix of black and red.
You play as Fawkes, a prisoner that gets a task to fulfill. After doing some stuff the voice tells you that you have now earned a ten day reduction on your prison sentence and now only 9113 days remain. A simple sentence that does so much for the setup of the story. Love it.
Returnal meets Doom, but well done this time. Recommended.

Command your forces to victory in Ashes of the Singularity II. A classical RTS.
Capture different areas to extend your base and to get more resource gathering nodes to build more units.
Each of the areas has it's own build queue which is different and makes the game focused more on the macro aspect rather than the hard micro of the many individual units. A decision I like.
Not sure why you can't set specific rally point for the different factories. That should be a stable in any RTS no matter how macro focused it is.
Overall an alright game but not something I would like to play more than this demo and I don't hate RTS games.

Pass the Fear along, we a nearing the end.
Enter the Gungeon meets Hades in this cute roguelike. Find different guns and fight through the different rooms like in Hades, choosing where to go next.
The guns are quite varied and each one can be further adjusted with a muzzle that adds an additional effect. For example I found a dragon gun that shot a hundred bullets really fast before it needed to be reloaded. Then I added a lightning muzzle on top of it, that created lightning on each third bullet. Combos like that are cool.
Graphics are cute too and fit well with the tight gameplay. I dig it.

Are ants not fascinating? Eusocial insects are just so unique, I love them.
No wonder that I tried Ant Colony: Wild Forest as it's an game about ants.
Kind of like a city builder. Place down areas to dig, different rooms or what units should be created and the ants will get around to it. Eventually.
Create workers to gather food and bring it to the queen to build more ants. Find a balance in the economy while fighting off attacks and raid other insect bases with your warrior ants.
The demo is quite long and despite the game being a bit simple it's a one more mission type of game. The different ants that can be created are not set in stone but you choose them with different cards, making a deck of ants you want to bring into the mission.
The ant cards can also be upgraded to create stronger ants. Reminds me a bit of some mobile game mechanics that make you grind to beat the next level. This isn't candy crush, is it?
Hooked me for a while but it's a bit too simple to be fun for longer. You can also not tell your ants to defend properly and it gets a bit frustrating once you lose your workers for the third time because the warriors are just sitting in the nest, bing chilling while everyone outside gets murdered.

Hungry? Why not try Snacktorio then?
A game about making all kinds of snacks for different hungry monsters. The game starts windowed and the window's title says 'I have no mouth and I must feed'. Peak reference.
The game starts with an elaborate tutorial of little puzzles that explain the different mechanics. An amazing way to do a tutorial. Really, props to the devs, this is great.
The game is more or less a sideways Factorio. Only you are not making factories to create circuits, you are harvesting ingredients to make different snacks.
Create elaborate assembly likes that transport, cook, and prepare the different parts of the final product.
The recipes don't seem that simple either. It's far from just smashing two things together in an assembler.
Game could be a lot of fun but nothing that you get into by just checking out a demo for half an hour or so. Takes time figuring all of it out.
Nothing negative here, cool game.

51

From the makers of the amazing Star Vaders comes Dice Vaders. Balatro like fun. Numbers go up and the combos go plink plink plonk.
In Star Vaders we had to defend earth against an invading alien armada, but now we play as the aliens. Create the invading force of different aliens on a random grid and destroy the mech pilots from the first game.
The units get placed on a six by four grid. At the start only a few tiles at the bottom are filled but over time you can expand the area. Each turn you roll dice that trigger the corresponding column to score points.
But it's not only up to chance, as you can get rockets to adjust the rolled dice one up or one down. You can also get chrono tokens to reroll them entirely, but only those you want to. It has an amazing depth to it. In addition to the dice mechanics each column can also hold an artifact that adds an special effect.
The combos of different aliens and artifacts go absolute crazy. Between each round there is a shop where you can buy new units, artifacts, rockets, chrono tokens or other special things like the ability to reposition your units.
Those chrono tokens to reroll your dice? Yeah they can also reroll the shop.
Absolutely love this game, couldn't stop playing. Wishlist and waiting for release just like with Star Vaders. These devs rock.

Once you wake up again you find yourself stranded on a small island. What with ships getting destroyed and starting on islands this time?
Anyways, do the usual crafting steps to get a workbench, tools and all of that. Build ships and explore the ocean.
Combat resolves around blocking and isn't too bad. According to the steam page it's souls like but I didn't really see that. Maybe I missed the dodge button though. They say this also has bosses and stuff.
Game is fun but in the end it's still a survival game and I'm so bored of them. Survival overload. If I wanted to play one I would play Minecraft again.

Edmund McMillen. You little fucker. You made a shit of piece with your trash Mewgenics. It's fucking bad this trash game. I will become back my money. I hope you will in your next time a cow; on a trash farm, you sucker.

The whole Isaac madness of items and combinations. And not only that. McMillen also added Cat breeding. Inbreed your little nuggets until you get super cats to take on a run.

Clair Obscur: Expedition 33. What’s there to say?
Actually, there is a lot. I could probably write a few pages about this game, but to keep it short: This game is perfect.

Another roguelike I recently added to my library is Slay the Spire 2. A slay the spire like deckbuilder.
Wait, it's not a STS like, this is STS! The original deckbuilder is back and so far it kept that magic of the first one going. A few things I noticed are that the early game and some specific normal monsters are way harder than in the first game. Maybe this is just early access stuff that will get fixed once the full version gets released.
At this point I have played for around twenty hours and can say the game is a lot of fun. However it can be very frustrating to encounter that one enemy or boss that counters your deck late in the run, but once you get that deck like you wanted it to and can win the run that familiar feeling from the first one hits.
Despite all that, I really love that they added multiplayer. That might even be the best addition to the game. Playing with your friends, both of you building up your deck and influencing each other through special cards or just by applying debuffs on the enemies in the right order is a lot of fun. I don’t remember another deckbuilder that has multiplayer.
Is this the second first time from the second slay the spire? Mega Crit is one of the goats. I can’t say if I like this or Mewgenics more, but at the end of the day it’s simply two cakes at once. More than I can eat and I have so many more cakes in the storage.

This Next Fest was amazing. Instead of piles of crap as usual this time around there were many promising demos. Especially these ones are worth a look:

  • The Eternal Life of Goldman, an amazing looking jump and run with fun cane mechanics
  • Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection, pokemon but with monsters from Monster Hunter
  • Far Far West, Helldivers but with cowboys and skeletons
  • REPLACED, 2D Platformer with stunning art
  • Gambonanza, amazing chess roguelike with fun mechanics
  • The Piper of Dawn, farming simulator with visual novel length characters dialogues

  • Luna Abyss, 3D bullet hell shooter
  • Pass the Fear, bullet-hell shooter like Hades
  • Dice Vaders, invade earth and score big to destroy humanity

These games are also good but not as amazing as the others and they are a bit more niche, so I don't think as many people will like them.

Banger Next Fest. I hope you too found time to test some of the demos and had some fun. Who knows, maybe some of the demos listed here may still be available, so go and check them out.
